How It Works

This site uses the Papaparse Library to read available data from the US Census Bureau, the New York Times' Coronavirus Database, and calculations from rt.live to calculate "R" values. I calculate the 7-day running average of daily new cases for your area, then assume each person infects "R" other people (based on your local R value), and assume each new infection is contagious but pre-symptomatic for 3 days. I also assume infections are undercounted by a factor of 5, based on published articles on fatality rates, excess deaths, and serology studies. Cases may be undercounted by more or less depending on the ease of getting tested in your area.

Based on the probability that one person is infected and contagious in your area, you can calculate the probability that any one of "n" people in the room with you is contagious.

This product is offered for information and discussion, and is not meant to replace sound decision making and public health expertise during a pandemic.
